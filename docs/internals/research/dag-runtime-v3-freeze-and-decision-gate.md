DAG Runtime V3 closes as a fidelity-and-experiment helper phase on top of a frozen DAG V2 kernel.

The key outcome is `freeze_and_reclassify`:

- DAG kernel pressure remains frozen.
- Recipe/helper work remains active.
- Optimize consumption is ready through comparison packets.
- RL work is ready to resume through bounded export-slice definitions.
- DARWIN remains outside the DAG runtime boundary.
- Higher-fidelity benchmark and evaluator work matters more than new DAG runtime shape.

This tranche does not add new runtime or schema primitives. Instead, it packages the evidence produced by the RSA tranche, the PaCoRe tranche, and the cross-paper composition tranche into one final decision gate.

The decision gate is intentionally narrow:

- it records the remaining pressure by owner
- it states whether a new DAG phase should open immediately
- it keeps the answer grounded in repeated-shape evidence rather than ambition alone

The current result is that `DAG V4` should not open now. The next meaningful work is outside the DAG kernel:

- deeper benchmark and evaluator fidelity
- recipe/helper refinement
- optimize-side consumption
- RL-side export and training overlays
- future DARWIN-side orchestration when outer-loop pressure actually appears

This preserves the intended V3 boundary:

- no new DAG public nouns
- no scheduler or state-family expansion
- no RL frameworkization inside DAG
- no DARWIN-lite campaign semantics

If future work reopens DAG planning, it should do so only after repeated evidence shows that the remaining pressure cannot stay helper-local or adapter-local.
